## KaTeX is working!

You can now write beautiful mathematical expressions in your blog posts.

### Inline Math

You can render inline math by wrapping your LaTeX code in single dollar signs. For example, the famous Pythagorean theorem can be written as $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$. Another example is the inline form of the Euler's identity, $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$.

### Block Math

For more complex or display-style equations, you can use double dollar signs to create a math block. This will center the equation on its own line.

Here's the Euler's identity again, but this time as a block equation:

$$
e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0
$$

And here is the quadratic formula:

$$
x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
$$

You can now use this syntax in any of your markdown files. Enjoy writing beautiful math!
